# Thong Nhat Hospital — Infectious Diseases Department

## Overview

Thong Nhat Hospital (Bệnh viện Thống Nhất) is a major general hospital in Ho Chi Minh City with a strong reputation in internal medicine. The **Infectious Diseases Department** handles a high volume of cases, providing an excellent learning environment for understanding the diverse spectrum of infections encountered in tropical and subtropical regions.

## Clinical Experience

My 6-week rotation exposed me to both common and rare infectious diseases, emphasizing the importance of systematic clinical reasoning and evidence-based antimicrobial therapy.

### Key Areas of Practice

- **Tropical Infections**: Dengue fever, typhoid, leptospirosis, and parasitic infections common in the Mekong Delta region
- **Respiratory Infections**: Community-acquired pneumonia, tuberculosis screening and management, and COVID-19 follow-up care
- **Hepatitis Management**: Chronic hepatitis B and C monitoring, liver function assessment, and antiviral treatment protocols
- **HIV/AIDS Care**: Antiretroviral therapy management, opportunistic infection prophylaxis, and patient counseling

### Notable Cases

1. **Disseminated Tuberculosis** — Involved in the workup of a patient with prolonged fever, weight loss, and multi-organ involvement. Learned the diagnostic approach including acid-fast bacilli smear, GeneXpert, and imaging interpretation.

2. **Severe Dengue with Shock Syndrome** — Managed a critical case requiring ICU-level fluid resuscitation and close hemodynamic monitoring. This case highlighted the importance of recognizing warning signs early and following WHO fluid management guidelines.

3. **Hepatitis B Reactivation** — Followed a patient who developed hepatitis B reactivation during immunosuppressive therapy. Gained understanding of screening protocols and prophylactic antiviral therapy.

## Skills Developed

- Systematic approach to fever of unknown origin
- Interpretation of serological tests and blood cultures
- Understanding of antimicrobial stewardship principles
- Infection control practices and personal protective equipment usage
- Patient education on disease prevention and transmission

## Reflections

The Infectious Diseases rotation at Thong Nhat Hospital was one of the most intellectually stimulating experiences in my clinical training. The department's emphasis on systematic diagnostic reasoning — starting from epidemiological exposure and clinical presentation to targeted laboratory investigations — has fundamentally shaped how I approach clinical problems.

Working in this department during the post-pandemic era also gave me a deeper appreciation for public health measures and the critical role that infectious disease specialists play in hospital infection control programs.
