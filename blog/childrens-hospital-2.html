Children's Hospital 2 — Pediatrics Department

Overview

Children's Hospital 2 (Bệnh viện Nhi đồng 2) is one of the largest and most well-known pediatric hospitals in Ho Chi Minh City, serving as a major referral center for the southern region of Vietnam. My rotation in the Pediatrics Department was an invaluable experience that shaped my understanding of child healthcare.

Clinical Experience

During my 8-week rotation, I was exposed to a wide range of pediatric conditions, from common childhood illnesses to complex cases requiring multidisciplinary management.

Key Areas of Practice

  • General Pediatrics: Assessment and management of febrile illnesses, respiratory infections, and gastrointestinal disorders in children
  • Neonatology: Observation of newborn care, neonatal jaundice management, and preterm infant monitoring
  • Pediatric Emergency: Triage and initial assessment of acute pediatric presentations, including febrile seizures and dehydration
  • Growth & Development: Monitoring developmental milestones and nutritional assessments

Notable Cases

  1. Kawasaki Disease — Participated in the diagnosis and management of a 3-year-old patient presenting with prolonged fever, bilateral conjunctival injection, and cervical lymphadenopathy. Learned the importance of early recognition and timely IVIG administration.

  2. Nephrotic Syndrome — Followed a case of a 5-year-old with generalized edema and proteinuria. Gained experience in monitoring treatment response to corticosteroids.

  3. Dengue Fever with Warning Signs — Managed fluid resuscitation protocols in a pediatric dengue case, learning the critical importance of monitoring hematocrit trends and platelet counts.

Skills Developed

  • Pediatric history taking and physical examination techniques
  • Age-appropriate communication with children and their families
  • Interpretation of pediatric laboratory values and growth charts
  • Management of common pediatric emergencies
  • Understanding of vaccination schedules and preventive care

Reflections

Working at Children's Hospital 2 taught me that pediatrics is not just about treating diseases — it's about understanding the whole child in the context of their family and development. The experience reinforced my belief that compassionate communication with both the child and their parents is as important as clinical knowledge.

The high volume of patients and diverse case mix at this hospital provided an excellent training ground for developing clinical judgment and efficiency in patient assessment.
